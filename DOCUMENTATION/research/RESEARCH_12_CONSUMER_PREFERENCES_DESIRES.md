# Consumer Preferences & Desires

## Dining Preferences

### Restaurant Type Preferences

#### Casual Dining Dominance
- **Most Popular Choice**: 69% of diners prefer casual dining in 2024 (up from 63% in 2023)
- **Reasons**: Balance of quality, atmosphere, and value
- **Growth Trend**: Continued growth in casual dining preference
- **Demographic Appeal**: Broad appeal across age groups
- **Frequency**: Higher frequency of visits to casual dining
- **Social Aspect**: Good for socializing and groups

#### Fast Food Growth
- **Increasing Preference**: 27% seek out fast food most often (up from 16% in 2023)
- **Reasons**: Speed, convenience, and lower cost
- **Solo Dining**: Correlated with increase in solo dining
- **Value Focus**: Smaller average bill size
- **Less Tipping**: Lower tipping expectations
- **Lunch Preference**: Popular for lunch occasions

#### Fine Dining Preferences
- **Special Occasions**: Preferred for celebrations and special events
- **Experience Focus**: Emphasis on unique experiences
- **Quality Expectations**: Highest expectations for food and service
- **Price Acceptance**: Willing to pay premium for experience
- **Lower Frequency**: Less frequent but higher value visits
- **Emotional Connection**: Strong emotional connection to experiences

### Dining Occasion Preferences

#### Social Dining
- **Group Dining**: Parties of 6+ up 8% year-over-year
- **Holiday Dining**: 55% plan to group dine over holidays
- **Social Connection**: Primary driver for restaurant choice
- **Celebration**: 86% prefer dine-in for special occasions
- **Atmosphere Importance**: Social atmosphere critical
- **Group Size Trends**: Both solo and large group dining increasing

#### Solo Dining Growth
- **Increasing Trend**: 21% typically dine alone (up from 18%)
- **Frequency**: 29% dine alone weekly or more often
- **Generational Difference**: 49% of Gen Z and 46% of Millennials dine alone weekly
- **Comfort Factors**: Booth seating, good atmosphere, not too loud
- **Acceptance**: Growing social acceptance of solo dining
- **Restaurant Adaptation**: More restaurants accommodating solo diners

#### Mid-Week Dining
- **Wednesday Trend**: Wednesday dining up 11% year-over-year
- **Week Break**: 41% dine out Wednesday to break up the week
- **Planning**: 43% plan to dine out Wednesday during the week
- **Value**: Often better value and less crowded
- **Routine**: Part of weekly routine for many
- **Restaurant Opportunity**: Opportunity to fill mid-week capacity

### Menu Preferences

#### Flavor and Cuisine Preferences
- **Adventure Seeking**: 61% plan to try new restaurants in 2025
- **Cultural Diversity**: Interest in diverse cuisines
- **Third Culture Cuisine**: Growing interest in fusion and authentic interpretations
- **Comfort Food**: Continued love for comfort foods (burgers, etc.)
- **Seafood**: Growing interest in seafood, especially raw bars
- **Vegetable Focus**: Cabbage emerging as new trendy vegetable

#### Dietary Preference Trends
- **Plant-Based Interest**: Growing interest in plant-forward meals
- **Health Conscious**: Increased focus on healthy options
- **Dietary Accommodations**: Expectation for various dietary needs
- **Allergen Awareness**: Higher awareness and accommodation needs
- **Gluten-Free**: Continued strong demand for gluten-free options
- **Customization**: Desire for meal customization

#### Menu Item Preferences
- **Burger Renaissance**: Return of the pub burger and quality burgers
- **Snacktails**: Cocktails with substantial garnishes
- **Omakase Evolution**: Expanding to affordable and speakeasy concepts
- **Cocktail Innovation**: New cocktail trends (Amaretto Sour predicted for 2025)
- **Non-Alcoholic Excellence**: Demand for great non-alcoholic options
- **Small Plates**: Preference for sharing and variety

### Service Preferences

#### Human Interaction Preferences
- **Staff Recognition**: Nearly half value being remembered by staff most
- **Personal Service**: Preference for human over automated service
- **Knowledgeable Staff**: Value staff knowledge and recommendations
- **Authentic Interactions**: Desire for genuine, not scripted, interactions
- **Relationship Building**: Value building relationships with staff
- **Emotional Connection**: Emotional connection drives loyalty

#### Technology Service Preferences
- **Convenience**: Technology valued for convenience
- **Efficiency**: Appreciate technology that speeds service
- **Personalization**: Expect personalized digital experiences
- **Integration**: Want seamless technology integration
- **Choice**: Want choice between human and technology
- **Balance**: Balance of high-tech and high-touch

### Atmosphere Preferences

#### Vibe-Focused Dining
- **Vibe Importance**: 25% say "overall vibe" is top factor in choosing restaurant
- **Vibe-Only Visits**: 23% visit cocktail/wine bars "just for the vibes"
- **Social Atmosphere**: Good for conversation and socializing
- **Comfort**: Comfortable and welcoming environment
- **Aesthetics**: Instagram-worthy and visually appealing
- **Music**: Appropriate music and noise levels

#### Physical Environment Preferences
- **Seating Preferences**: Booth seating preferred (60% for "yappers")
- **Lighting**: Appropriate lighting for occasion
- **Temperature**: Comfortable temperature control
- **Cleanliness**: High expectations for cleanliness
- **Layout**: Efficient and comfortable layout
- **Privacy**: Balance of privacy and social atmosphere

## Generational Preference Differences

### Gen Z Preferences
- **Technology-First**: Comfortable with and expect technology integration
- **Social Media**: Instagram-worthy experiences important
- **Values Alignment**: Strong alignment with personal values
- **Authenticity**: Value authentic experiences
- **Sustainability**: Strong preference for sustainable practices
- **Solo Dining**: Higher comfort with solo dining

### Millennial Preferences
- **Experience-Focused**: Value unique and memorable experiences
- **Quality**: High expectations for quality
- **Convenience**: Balance of quality and convenience
- **Social Responsibility**: Expect social responsibility
- **Loyalty Programs**: Value loyalty and rewards
- **Adventure**: Willing to try new experiences

### Boomer Preferences

- **Physical Menus**: Strong preference for physical menus
- **Personal Interaction**: Value personal interaction
- **Consistency**: Highly value consistency
- **Value**: Strong focus on value
- **Comfort**: Prefer familiar experiences

## Conclusion

Consumer preferences and desires are complex and multifaceted, varying by demographic, occasion, and individual values. The most successful restaurants understand these preferences and create experiences that resonate with their target customers. Key trends include the desire for authentic experiences, personalization, convenience, and alignment with personal values.

Understanding these preferences enables restaurants to tailor their offerings, service, and marketing to effectively meet customer desires and build lasting loyalty. The goal is to create experiences that not only meet but exceed customer desires, creating emotional connections and memorable experiences.
