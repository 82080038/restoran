# Regulatory & Legal Requirements for Restaurants

## Overview

Restaurants operate in one of the most heavily regulated industries. Beyond food safety and health regulations, operators must navigate a complex tapestry of labor laws, tax requirements, licensing, and employee protections. The regulatory landscape varies significantly by jurisdiction, making compliance particularly challenging for multi-location operators.

## Labor Law Compliance

### Federal Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)

#### Minimum Wage Requirements
- **Federal Minimum Wage**: $7.25/hour (as of 2025)
- **Tip Credit**: Up to $5.12 tip credit, allowing tipped employees to be paid $2.13/hour if tips make up the difference
- **Highest Rate Rule**: Must pay whichever is highest - federal, state, or local minimum wage
- **No-Tip Credit States**: California, Washington, Oregon, Alaska, Minnesota, Montana, Nevada, and DC require full minimum wage for all employees including tipped staff

#### Overtime Requirements
- **Standard Overtime**: 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40 per workweek
- **Double Time**: Twice regular rate for certain situations (varies by state)
- **Daily Overtime**: Some states require overtime after 8 hours in a day
- **Seventh Day Overtime**: Some states require overtime on 7th consecutive day of work

#### Tip Credit Requirements
- **Written Notice**: Must provide written notice before taking tip credit
- **80/20 Rule**: Tipped employees cannot spend more than 20% of time on non-tipped duties while receiving tip credit
- **30-Minute Rule**: Cannot perform non-tipped tasks for more than 30 continuous minutes
- **Tip Pool Restrictions**: Only employees who customarily and regularly receive tips can participate
- **Manager Prohibition**: Managers, supervisors, and owners prohibited from tip pools
- **Retroactive Liability**: One ineligible participant can invalidate entire tip credit retroactively

#### Record Keeping Requirements
- **Time Records**: Minimum 2 years retention per FLSA
- **I-9 Records**: 3 years from hire or 1 year post-termination
- **Pay Stubs**: Must provide detailed pay stubs
- **Break Records**: Documentation of meal and rest breaks
- **Tip Records**: Documentation of tip allocations

### State and Local Labor Laws

#### Predictive Scheduling
- **Fair Workweek Ordinances**: Chicago requires 14 days' notice for schedule changes
- **Schedule Change Premiums**: Premiums required for last-minute adjustments
- **Right to Rest**: Some jurisdictions require rest between shifts
- **Availability Rights**: Employee rights to set availability preferences

#### Break Requirements
- **Rest Breaks**: Paid 10-minute rest break for every 4 hours (varies by state)
- **Meal Breaks**: Unpaid 30-minute meal break if work more than 5 hours
- **Waiver Options**: Some states allow meal break waivers for 6-hour shifts
- **Compensable Breaks**: If employee works through break, time must be compensated

#### Child Labor Regulations
- **Under 14**: Generally prohibited from non-agricultural employment
- **14-15 Years Old**: Limited hours and occupations
  - School days: Max 3 hours/day, 18 hours/week
  - Non-school days: Max 8 hours/day, 40 hours/week
  - Time restrictions: 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. (9 p.m. June 1-Labor Day)
- **16-17 Years Old**: Unlimited hours in non-hazardous occupations
- **Hazardous Occupations**: Prohibited occupations for minors under 18

#### Pay Transparency
- **Pay Range Disclosure**: Required in job postings in CA, CO, CT, DC, HI, IL, MD, MN, NV, NJ, NY, RI, VT, WA
- **Internal Postings**: Some jurisdictions require internal pay ranges
- **Employee Rights**: Right to learn pay range for current position
- **Benefit Disclosure**: Some laws require benefit information alongside pay ranges

### Common Violations

#### Misclassification
- **Independent Contractor Misclassification**: AB55 in California addresses employment status
- **Employment Status Portal**: Resources for determining proper classification
- **Penalties**: Significant penalties for misclassification
- **Retroactive Liability**: Back wages and penalties for misclassified workers

#### Wage Violations
- **Minimum Wage Violations**: Not paying applicable minimum wage
- **Overtime Violations**: Not paying proper overtime
- **Tip Credit Violations**: Improper tip credit application
- **Off-the-Clock Work**: Not compensating all hours worked

#### Record Keeping Violations
- **Inadequate Records**: Not maintaining required records for 4 years
- **Incomplete Records**: Missing required information
- **Record Retention**: Not retaining records for required period
- **Minor Records**: Special requirements for minor employees

## Business Licensing & Permits

### Federal Requirements

#### Employer Identification Number (EIN)
- **Requirement**: Required for all businesses with employees
- **Application**: IRS Form SS-4
- **Use**: Tax reporting and identification
- **Timing**: Before hiring first employee

#### Federal Tax Registration
- **Federal Tax ID**: Required for tax purposes
- **Employer Taxes**: Federal unemployment tax (FUTA)
- **Social Security**: Employer portion of Social Security tax
- **Medicare**: Employer portion of Medicare tax

### State Requirements

#### Business License
- **Requirement**: Required to operate any business
- **Variation**: Varies by state and municipality
- **Renewal**: Annual renewal typically required
- **Fees**: Vary by location and business type

#### Sales Tax Permit
- **Requirement**: Required for selling taxable goods
- **Registration**: Must register with state tax authority
- **Collection**: Must collect sales tax on taxable sales
- **Reporting**: Regular reporting and remittance required

#### Employer Registration
- **State Unemployment**: State unemployment insurance registration
- **Workers' Compensation**: Required in most states
- **State Withholding**: State income tax withholding registration
- **New Hire Reporting**: Required new hire reporting

### Local Requirements

#### Local Business License
- **City/County License**: Additional local licensing often required
- **Zoning Compliance**: Must comply with local zoning laws
- **Health Permit**: Local health department permit
- **Fire Department Permit**: Fire safety inspection and permit

#### Food Service License
- **Health Department**: Required from local health department
- **Inspection**: Premises inspection required
- **Food Safety**: Food safety manager certification often required
- **Renewal**: Annual renewal typically required

#### Alcohol License
- **Liquor License**: Required for serving alcohol
- **Types**: Various types (beer/wine, full liquor, etc.)
- **Restrictions**: Hours, days, and service restrictions
- **Cost**: Can be expensive and limited in availability

#### Building Permits
- **Occupancy Permit**: Certificate of occupancy required
- **Building Health Permit**: Building inspection for health compliance
- **Signage Permit**: Required for exterior signage
- **Parking**: Parking requirements may apply

## Tax Compliance

### Sales Tax

#### Taxable Sales
- **Food and Beverages**: Most sales of food and beverages are taxable
- **On-Premises Consumption**: Generally taxable whether hot or cold
- **Heated Food**: Generally taxable whether sold by restaurant or supermarket
- **Surcharge**: Surcharges added to taxable sales are also taxable

#### Exemptions
- **To-Go Sales**: May be exempt depending on 80-80 rule
- **Grocery Items**: Some grocery items may be exempt
- **Resale**: Purchases for resale are exempt
- **Non-Profit**: Sales to non-profit organizations for fundraising

#### 80-80 Rule
- **Test**: More than 80% of gross receipts from food products AND more than 80% of food products are taxable
- **Location-Based**: Evaluated on location-by-location basis
- **Impact**: If met, all sales may be taxable
- **Documentation**: Must maintain adequate documentation

#### Record Keeping
- **Three Years**: Records must be kept for at least three years
- **Detail Required**: Must have enough detail to prove taxability
- **Guest Checks**: Sequentially numbered guest checks required
- **Cash Register Tapes**: Dated tapes required
- **POS Systems**: Must provide taxability detail for each transaction

### Use Tax
- **Out-of-State Purchases**: Due on purchases from out-of-state vendors
- **Inventory Removal**: Due on items removed from inventory for business use
- **Reporting**: Report under "Purchases Subject to Use Tax"
- **Calculation**: Based on purchase price

### Payroll Taxes
- **Federal Withholding**: Federal income tax withholding
- **State Withholding**: State income tax withholding (where applicable)
- **Social Security**: 6.2% employer portion
- **Medicare**: 1.45% employer portion
- **FUTA**: Federal unemployment tax
- **SUTA**: State unemployment tax

### Income Tax
- **Business Income Tax**: Federal and state income tax on business profits
- **Pass-Through Entities**: Different treatment for different entity types
- **Estimated Taxes**: Quarterly estimated tax payments required
- **Deductions**: Ordinary and necessary business expenses deductible

## Conclusion

Restaurant regulatory compliance is complex and constantly evolving. The most successful operators treat compliance as a core business function, investing in systems, training, and expert support to ensure compliance across all jurisdictions where they operate. The cost of non-compliance - in fines, penalties, back wages, and reputational damage - far exceeds the cost of proactive compliance management.

For multi-location operators, centralized compliance management with local adaptation is essential. Technology solutions can significantly reduce the compliance burden, but they must be complemented by strong processes, training, and expert support.
