# Consumer Behavior & Dining Trends

## Current Dining Behavior Patterns

### Dine-In vs Takeout Preferences

#### Shift Back to Dine-In
- **Dine-In Preference**: 55% prefer dining at restaurants over takeout/delivery in 2024 (up from 43% in 2023)
- **Primary Drivers**: Atmosphere and socializing are top reasons for preferring dine-in
- **Pandemic Recovery**: Pandemic-era habits receding, social and experiential draw reasserting
- **Occasion-Based**: Special occasions still drive dine-in preference (86% for special occasions)
- **Social Connection**: Time to socialize with others remains key driver
- **Experience Value**: Valuing the full restaurant experience

#### Takeout and Delivery Behavior
- **Weekly Takeout**: 47% pick up takeout at least once a week
- **Weekly Drive-Thru**: 42% use drive-thru at least once a week
- **Weekly Delivery**: 37% order delivery at least once a week
- **Age Variation**: Younger adults most frequent users of off-premises
- **Convenience Focus**: Driven by convenience and time constraints
- **Channel Integration**: 74% say dine-in visit led to later delivery from same restaurant

### Spending Behavior

#### Increased Spending
- **Monthly Spend Increase**: Average monthly spend $191 in 2024 vs $166 in 2023
- **Inflation Impact**: Some increase attributed to menu price inflation
- **Gender Difference**: Men spending 19% more per month than women in 2024
- **Income Variation**: Lower income households cutting back, higher income maintaining habits
- **Value Expectation**: Spending more but expecting value for money
- **Quality Focus**: Willing to pay more for quality experience

#### Tipping Behavior
- **Stable Habits**: More than half report no change in tipping habits
- **Pressure Sensitivity**: Don't like pressure to tip more
- **Service Quality**: Tips still tied to service quality perception
- **Channel Variation**: Tipping varies by channel (dine-in vs takeout)
- **Generational Differences**: Tipping habits vary by generation
- **Economic Pressure**: Economic pressures influencing tipping behavior

### Timing and Frequency Behavior

#### Dining Frequency
- **Weekly Diners**: Number dining out weekly or more increased from previous year
- **Lunch Growth**: Increased preference for lunch dining (cheaper than dinner)
- **Mid-Week Growth**: Wednesday dining up 11% year-over-year
- **Routine Building**: Dining becoming part of weekly routine
- **Seasonal Variation**: Seasonal patterns in dining frequency
- **Economic Sensitivity**: Economic conditions affecting frequency

#### Wait Time Tolerance
- **Increased Patience**: Willing to wait up to 26 minutes without reservation (vs 20 minutes in 2023)
- **Communication Importance**: Being kept informed reduces frustration
- **Reservation Preference**: Preference for reservations to avoid waits
- **Value Trade-off**: Willing to wait for perceived value
- **Occasion Dependency**: Special occasions increase wait tolerance
- **Technology Impact**: Technology helping manage wait times

## Emerging Behavioral Trends

### Solo Dining Trend
- **Growth**: 21% typically dine alone (up from 18%)
- **Frequency**: 29% dine alone weekly or more often
- **Generational**: 49% of Gen Z and 46% of Millennials dine alone weekly
- **Comfort Factors**: Booth seating, good atmosphere, not too loud
- **Social Acceptance**: Growing acceptance of solo dining
- **Restaurant Adaptation**: More restaurants accommodating solo diners

### Experiential Dining
- **Experience Growth**: 42% more interested in experiential dining in 2025 vs 2024
- **Booking Increase**: OpenTable Experiences bookings up 27% year-over-year
- **Adventure Seeking**: 61% plan to try new restaurants in 2025
- **Multi-Course Interest**: Growing interest in tasting menus and omakase
- **Educational Component**: Interest in cooking classes and experiences
- **Differentiation**: Seeking unique and differentiating experiences

### Group Dining Resurgence
- **Large Groups**: Parties of 6+ up 8% year-over-year
- **Holiday Planning**: 55% plan to group dine over holidays
- **Future Intent**: 43% plan to group dine more frequently in 2025
- **Social Connection**: Group dining for social connection
- **Celebration Focus**: Groups for celebrations and events
- **Restaurant Adaptation**: Restaurants adapting for larger groups

### "Vibe" Dining
- **Vibe Importance**: 25% say "overall vibe" is top factor in choosing restaurant
- **Vibe-Only Visits**: 23% visit cocktail/wine bars "just for the vibes"
- **Atmosphere Focus**: Prioritizing atmosphere over other factors
- **Social Media**: Instagram-worthy experiences important
- **Aesthetic Investment**: Restaurants investing in aesthetics
- **Experience Economy**: Part of broader experience economy trend

## Technology-Driven Behavior

### Mobile Ordering Behavior
- **App Usage**: 57% used mobile app for off-premises order in past 6 months
- **Generational Split**: 74% of Millennials, 65% of Gen Z, 36% of Boomers
- **Preference**: 35% prefer mobile app for ordering
- **In-Person Alternative**: 32% prefer in-person ordering
- **Channel Mixing**: Customers use multiple ordering channels
- **Convenience Driver**: Convenience primary driver for app usage

### Digital Menu Behavior
- **Physical Menu Preference**: 90% prefer print menus over QR codes (up from 74% in 2023)
- **Generational Agreement**: 90% of Gen Z prefer paper menus (up from 69% in 2023)
- **Pandemic Rejection**: Many tried QR codes during pandemic and found lacking
- **Tactile Experience**: Preference for physical menu experience
- **Ease of Use**: Physical menus perceived as easier to use
- **Technology Fatigue**: Some technology fatigue post-pandemic

### AI Discovery Behavior
- **AI Usage**: 22% have used AI tools like ChatGPT to help choose restaurant
- **Discovery Signal**: AI becoming new discovery channel
- **Restaurant Citations**: Restaurant sites account for 4% of sources AI tools cite
- **Information Seeking**: Using AI for comparison and information
- **Early Adoption**: Early adopters using AI for restaurant discovery
- **Growth Potential**: Significant growth potential for AI discovery

### Multi-Channel Behavior
- **Fluid Movement**: 64% prefer one app for delivery, pickup, and reservations
- **Channel Switching**: 74% dine-in led to later delivery from same restaurant
- **Reverse Flow**: 62% delivery led to later dine-in
- **Occasion-Based**: Channel choice based on occasion
- **Consistency Desire**: Expect consistency across channels
- **Integration Need**: Need for integrated multi-channel experience

## Conclusion

Consumer behavior in dining continues to evolve, driven by technology, changing demographics, and shifting values. Key trends include the return to dine-in for social experiences, growth in solo dining, increased interest in experiential dining, and strong preference for integrated multi-channel experiences. Understanding these behavioral patterns enables restaurants to create experiences that resonate with modern consumers and build lasting loyalty.

The most successful restaurants anticipate and adapt to these behavioral trends, creating experiences that align with how consumers want to dine today and will want to dine tomorrow.
